The importance of tangibility in education

Tangibility is important in S.T.E.A.M. because it allows us to see and interact with the products and solutions that are developed through this methodology. S.T.E.A.M. is an interdisciplinary approach that emphasizes practical application, and tangible products provide a concrete way to demonstrate the effectiveness of S.T.E.A.M. solutions.

For example, when developing a new technology product, a tangible prototype can be developed to test its functionality and identify any potential issues or improvements that need to be made. This allows for iterative improvements and refinement of the product until it is ready for market.

Additionally, tangibility allows for more effective communication and collaboration among team members and stakeholders. Tangible prototypes and products provide a shared language and visual representation that can be easily understood by all parties involved. This can help to align expectations and ensure that everyone is on the same page when it comes to the development of a solution.

Finally, tangibility also plays an important role in engaging and inspiring learners, particularly in S.T.E.A.M. education. Tangible products and experiments allow students to see the practical application of S.T.E.A.M. concepts and inspire them to pursue careers in these fields. Seeing the tangible results of their efforts can also boost their confidence and motivate them to continue exploring S.T.E.A.M. concepts and developing new solutions.

In summary, tangibility is an important aspect of S.T.E.A.M. because it allows for practical application, effective communication and collaboration, and engagement and inspiration of learners.
